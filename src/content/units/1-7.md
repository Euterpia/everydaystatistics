A disease affects 1 in 10,000 people. A test for the disease is 99% accurate — meaning it correctly identifies 99% of sick people and correctly clears 99% of healthy ones. You test positive. What is the probability you have the disease?

Most people say "99%." The correct answer is approximately 1%. This unit explains why — through the concept of the base rate — and introduces Bayes' theorem as the formal tool for thinking about evidence correctly.
