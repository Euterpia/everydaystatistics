The Sally Clark case: a mother convicted of murdering two infant sons, partly on the basis of expert testimony that the probability of two successive cot deaths in the same family was 1 in 73 million. This was not the relevant probability. The relevant probability was: given two infant deaths in this family, what is the probability of double cot death versus double murder? The prior probability of murder was never considered.
