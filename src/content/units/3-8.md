Simpson's Paradox is not an edge case. It has appeared in analyses of gender discrimination at UC Berkeley, kidney stone treatment effectiveness, and batting averages in baseball. In each case, the direction of the effect reversed when the data was disaggregated. Pooling groups without checking for confounding by group composition is an error with large real-world consequences.
