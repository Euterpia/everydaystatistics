A drug reduces the risk of heart attack "by 36%." This sounds transformative. If the baseline risk in the study population was 3%, the absolute risk reduction is approximately 1 percentage point — meaning you would need to treat 100 people for one person to benefit. The other 99 receive no benefit and are exposed to the drug's side effects.

This is not dishonest in a legal sense. It is dishonest in every sense that matters. This unit teaches the distinction between absolute and relative risk, introduces the Number Needed to Treat, and identifies the tell in every pharmaceutical advertisement you will ever read.
