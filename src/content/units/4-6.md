In a controlled experiment, participants were asked to estimate the percentage of African countries in the United Nations after watching a roulette wheel spin to either 10 or 65. The roulette number was arbitrary and participants knew it. Their subsequent estimates were nonetheless anchored to it: the "65" group gave substantially higher estimates than the "10" group.

If an arbitrary number can move a factual estimate, a strategically chosen opening price can move almost anything.
