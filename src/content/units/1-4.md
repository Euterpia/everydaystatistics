P(A|B) — the probability of A given that B has occurred — is not the same as P(B|A). This distinction sounds trivial. It is responsible for wrongful convictions, medical misdiagnoses, and systematic errors in how we interpret evidence.

This unit introduces conditional probability through concrete examples and prepares the ground for the full Bayesian treatment in Unit 1.7.
