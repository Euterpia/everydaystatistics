When all trials of antidepressants registered with the FDA were analysed — including those not published — the effect size was considerably smaller than suggested by the published literature alone. The unpublished trials had null or negative results. They were not published. This is publication bias in its most consequential form: a systematic distortion of the evidence base that affects prescribing decisions affecting millions of patients.
