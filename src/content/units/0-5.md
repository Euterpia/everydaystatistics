A 20% increase in risk sounds alarming regardless of what the baseline is. A 20% increase on a 0.1% risk produces a risk of 0.12% — an increase of one person in a thousand. A 20% increase on a 10% risk produces a risk of 12% — a very different thing.

The same number means different things in different contexts. This unit introduces absolute risk, relative risk, and the baseline problem — and explains why emotional risk perception diverges systematically from mathematical risk.
