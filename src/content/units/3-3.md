A y-axis that starts at 96% instead of 0% makes a change from 96% to 97% look like a doubling. A dual-axis graph can make any two unrelated time series look correlated or anti-correlated by adjusting the scales independently. These are not edge cases. They are standard practice in political and financial communication.
