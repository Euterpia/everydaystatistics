The multiple comparisons problem is simple: if you test 20 independent hypotheses each at the 5% significance level, you expect one false positive by chance alone, even if none of the hypotheses are true. Testing 100 hypotheses produces five expected false positives. The practice of testing many things and reporting only the significant ones is not just bad science — it is structurally indistinguishable from fraud.
