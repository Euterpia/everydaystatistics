The normal distribution is not a law of nature. It is a mathematical approximation that fits many naturally occurring phenomena — but not all. When financial models assume normality in asset returns, they systematically underestimate the probability of extreme events. This is not a theoretical concern: it contributed materially to the 2008 financial crisis.
