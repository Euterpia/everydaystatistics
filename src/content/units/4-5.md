The original 1985 study found no evidence of the hot hand in basketball shooting. The finding was widely reported: streaks are an illusion. A 2018 reanalysis found a flaw in the original analysis — one that caused it to underestimate the true effect. The hot hand may be real. The lesson is not "the hot hand exists." The lesson is: results in dependent processes cannot be analysed using tools designed for independent events.
