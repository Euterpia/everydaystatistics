In the years after 9/11, Americans drove rather than flew — rationally responding to perceived risk. The additional road miles driven produced approximately 1,600 extra road deaths in the year following the attacks, compared to flying, which is 50 times safer per mile. The availability of the plane crash image killed more people than recalibrating to its actual risk would have done.
