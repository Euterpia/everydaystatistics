In criminal justice, we accept many false acquittals to avoid a single wrongful conviction. In a cancer screening programme, we accept many false positives to avoid missing a treatable tumour. The mathematics are the same; the ethics differ. Understanding Type I and Type II errors means understanding that there is no such thing as "more accurate" without specifying accurate in which direction.
