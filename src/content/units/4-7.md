Across many studies, when people say they are "90% confident" in an answer, they are correct approximately 70% of the time. The gap is not random noise — it is systematic overconfidence. Projects take longer than planned because the optimistic scenario is treated as the expected scenario. Experts are not immune: political forecasters and economic analysts show the same pattern.
