The question most researchers want to answer is: "Given this data, how probable is our hypothesis?" Null-hypothesis testing cannot answer this question. It can only answer: "If the null hypothesis were true, how improbable is this data?" Bayesian inference answers the right question — at the cost of requiring a prior probability, which introduces its own complications.

This unit presents both frameworks honestly, explains the prior problem, and explains when each is the appropriate tool.
