A roulette wheel has landed on red 26 consecutive times. The casino fills. People bet heavily on black, certain that the run must end. Each spin remains a 48.6% probability of red. The wheel has no memory of previous outcomes. The casino wins.

The gambler's fallacy is the belief that independent events self-correct. They do not. The law of large numbers operates over millions of trials, not within any individual sequence.
