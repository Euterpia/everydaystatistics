In World War II, Abraham Wald was asked to determine where to add armour to bomber aircraft, based on analysis of bullet holes in returning planes. His counterintuitive recommendation: armour the places with no bullet holes. The planes that were hit there did not return.

Survivorship bias is everywhere. The business books profile successful companies. The investment advertisements highlight the winning funds. The self-help memoirs are written by the people for whom the advice worked. The failures are silent.
