The expected value of a lottery ticket is the probability-weighted average of all possible prize outcomes. For almost every lottery, this number is well below the ticket price. Expected value does not tell you what will happen on any given ticket — it tells you what happens across millions of tickets. The lottery is a reliable tax on misunderstanding this distinction.
