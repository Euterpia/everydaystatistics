In 2015, the Open Science Collaboration attempted to replicate 100 published psychology studies. Fewer than 40% replicated successfully. This was not a surprise to statisticians who understood the structural incentives of academic publishing. It was a shock to everyone who had been reading the headlines.

The replication crisis does not mean science is broken. It means science as filtered through publication bias, small samples, p-hacking, and media simplification is systematically misleading. This unit explains each mechanism.
