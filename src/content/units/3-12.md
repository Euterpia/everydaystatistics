A mammography screening programme. Sensitivity 80%: it correctly identifies 80% of women who have breast cancer. Specificity 90%: it correctly clears 90% of women who do not. In a population where 1% of women screened have breast cancer, a positive result means an approximately 7.5% probability of actual cancer. The other 92.5% of positive results are false positives, leading to unnecessary biopsies, anxiety, and treatment.

This is not an argument against mammography. It is an argument for honest risk communication.
