A frequentist says the probability of a coin landing heads is 0.5 because if you flipped it a very large number of times, half would be heads. A Bayesian says the probability of a coin landing heads is 0.5 because that is their degree of belief in the outcome before the flip.

These are different ideas, and they produce different answers to questions like "what is the probability it will rain tomorrow?" — a question the frequentist framework cannot answer directly, because you cannot repeat tomorrow. This unit clarifies both interpretations and explains when each applies.
