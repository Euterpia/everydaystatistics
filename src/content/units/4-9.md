Statistical literacy, in its deepest form, is not the ability to compute a formula. It is the habit of asking: what is the base rate here? How much should this evidence update my belief? What is the prior? Am I using the inside view or the outside view?

A person who habitually asks these questions — even without ever calculating a posterior probability explicitly — is Bayesian in the sense that matters. That is what this curriculum has been building toward.
