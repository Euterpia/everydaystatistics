Ask someone to generate a random sequence of coin flips. They will avoid runs of the same outcome, because runs feel non-random. Real coin flip sequences contain runs, because randomness does not self-correct. The human-generated sequence is actually less random than the real one.

This unit establishes what randomness actually is — and what it is not. The clustering illusion, the hot hand, lottery "systems," and trading strategies built on chart patterns all rest on the same mistake this unit corrects.
