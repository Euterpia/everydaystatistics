A cab was involved in a hit-and-run at night. The city has two cab companies: Blue (85% of cabs) and Green (15%). A witness identifies the cab as Green. The witness is 80% reliable in such conditions. What is the probability the cab was Green?

Most people say 80%. The correct answer is 41%. The base rate — the prior probability that any cab involved is Green — does almost all the work, and almost everyone ignores it.
