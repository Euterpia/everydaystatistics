A p-value of 0.03 does not mean there is a 3% chance the result is due to chance. It does not mean there is a 97% probability that the hypothesis is true. It means: if the null hypothesis were true, the probability of seeing data at least as extreme as this is 3%.

This distinction is not semantic. The difference between what a p-value says and what most people think it says is the source of most misreported scientific findings.
